

[Tertullian, Against Marcion Book 5.20](https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/03125.htm)

> [!Quote] [Christ Nature]
> Of course the Marcionites suppose that they have the apostle on their side in the following passage in the matter of [Christ's](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/08374c.htm) substance — that in Him there was nothing but a phantom of flesh. For he says of [Christ](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/08374c.htm), that, being in the form of [God](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/06608a.htm), He thought it not [robbery](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/14564b.htm) to be equal with [God](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/06608a.htm); but emptied Himself, and took upon Him the _form_ of a servant, _not the reality_, and was made in the _likeness_ of [man](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/09580c.htm), _not a man_, and was found in _fashion_ as a [man](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/09580c.htm), [Philippians 2:6-7](https://www.newadvent.org/bible/phi002.htm#verse6) _not in his substance_, that is to say, his flesh; just as if to a substance there did not accrue both _form_ and _likeness_ and _fashion_. It is well for us that in another passage (the apostle) calls Christ the image of the invisible God. [Colossians 1:15](https://www.newadvent.org/bible/col001.htm#verse15) For will it not follow with equal force from that passage, that [Christ](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/08374c.htm) is not [truly](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/15073a.htm) [God](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/06608a.htm), because the apostle places Him in _the image_ of [God](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/06608a.htm), if, (as [Marcion](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/09645c.htm) contends,) He is not [truly](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/15073a.htm) man because of His having taken on Him _the form_ or _image_ of a man? For in both cases the [true](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/15073a.htm) substance will have to be excluded, if _image_ (or fashion) and _likeness_ and _form_ shall be claimed for a phantom. But since he is [truly](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/15073a.htm) [God](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/06608a.htm), as the Son of the [Father](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/06608a.htm), in His fashion and image, He has been already by the force of this conclusion determined to be [truly](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/15073a.htm) man, as the [Son of man](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/14144a.htm), found in the fashion and image of a [man](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/09580c.htm). For when he propounded Him as thus  _found_ in the manner of a [man](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/09580c.htm), he _in fact_ affirmed Him to be most certainly [human](https://www.newadvent.org/cathen/09580c.htm).
